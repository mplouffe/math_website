The unit circle is a cirlce with a radius of 1.

Being so simple, it's a great tool to talk about lengnths and angles.

Pythagoras

Pythagoras' Theorem says that for a right angled triangle, the square of the long side equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides:

x2 + y2 = 12

But 12 is 1 so:

x2 + y2 = 1

the equation of the unit circle

Since x = cos, and y = sin, we get:

(cos(Θ))2 + (sin(Θ))2 = 1

A useful "identity"
