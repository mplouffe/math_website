The Unit Circle is a circle with a radius of 1.

Being so simple, it's a great tool to talk about lengths and angles.

Pythagoras

Pythagoras' Theorem says that for a right angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (aka the long side) equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides:

a2 + b2 = c2

If we build a right angle triangle with the hypotenuse along the radius of the unit circle, we can observe some interesting properties. Since 12 is 1:

x2 + y2 = 1
The equation of the unit circle

Since x = cos, and y = sin, we get:

(cos(Θ))2 + (sin(Θ))2 = 1
A useful "identity"
